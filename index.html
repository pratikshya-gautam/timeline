Development of Hinduism Timeline Chart

  • 2500–1500 BCE
    Indus Valley Civilization Period
    The Indus Valley Civilization developed and flourished in the basin of the Indus River, with evidence of early Hindu practices.
    Development and Flourishing
    The Indus Valley Civilization, known for its advanced urban planning and culture, developed and flourished in the basin of the Indus River.
    Reference: https://www.britannica.com/topic/Indus-civilization

    Early Hindu Practices
    Evidence of early Hindu practices includes ritual baths and fire altars, indicating the origins of some Hindu traditions.
    Reference: https://www.worldhistory.org/Indus_Valley_Civilization/

  • 1500 BCE
    Vedic Period Begins
    Debate over Aryan migration versus cultural transformation thesis; the composition of the Rig Veda marks the start of the Vedic Age.
    Aryan Migration Theory
    The migration of Aryans into India is debated as either an invasion or a gradual cultural transformation.
    Reference: https://www.britannica.com/topic/Aryan

    Composition of the Rigveda
    The Rigveda, one of the oldest sacred texts of Hinduism, is composed, marking the beginning of the Vedic Age.
    Reference: https://www.britannica.com/topic/Rigveda

  • 800 BCE
    Upanishads Written
    The eleven major Upanishads are written, introducing key Hindu concepts like reincarnation, karma, and moksha.
    Composition of the Upanishads
    The Upanishads are composed, exploring concepts such as Brahman (the ultimate reality) and Atman (the self).
    Reference: https://www.britannica.com/topic/Upanishad

    Introduction of Reincarnation and Karma
    The concepts of reincarnation and karma are elaborated in the Upanishads, shaping Hindu beliefs about life and rebirth.
    Reference: https://psi-encyclopedia.spr.ac.uk/articles/reincarnation-and-karma

  • 600–500 BCE
    Emergence of Buddhism and Jainism
    Buddhism and Jainism break away from the main flow of Hinduism, introducing new spiritual and social ideas.
    Founding of Buddhism
    Buddhism is founded by Siddhartha Gautama (the Buddha), offering a new path to enlightenment and challenging the Vedic traditions.
    Reference: https://www.britannica.com/topic/Buddhism

    Founding of Jainism
    Jainism is founded by Mahavira, advocating non-violence (ahimsa) and asceticism as central principles.
    Reference: https://www.britannica.com/topic/Jainism

  • 500 BCE
    Epic and Puranic Texts
    The Epics and Puranas are written, marking the beginning of devotional Hinduism with a focus on deities like Shiva, Vishnu, and Devi.
    Composition of the Mahabharata and Ramayana
    The Mahabharata and Ramayana, two major epics of Hinduism, are composed, focusing on dharma and devotion to deities.
    Reference: https://www.britannica.com/topic/Mahabharata and https://www.britannica.com/topic/Ramayana

    Development of Puranas
    The Puranas are written, providing narratives about the gods, the creation of the universe, and genealogies of gods and sages.
    Reference: https://www.britannica.com/topic/Purana

  • 400 BCE–800 AD
    Hindu Response to Buddhism and Jainism
    Hinduism evolves in response to Buddhism and Jainism; the Gupta Empire fosters the development of Vaishnavism, Shaivism, and Shaktism.
    Rise of Vaishnavism, Shaivism, and Shaktism
    The Gupta Empire period sees the rise of major Hindu sects: Vaishnavism (devotion to Vishnu), Shaivism (devotion to Shiva), and Shaktism (devotion to the Divine Feminine).
    Reference: https://www.britannica.com/topic/Vaishnavism and https://www.britannica.com/topic/Shaivism

  • 326 BCE
    Alexander the Great’s Invasion
    Alexander’s invasion introduces Hellenic culture to India, influencing local traditions.
    Alexander’s Campaigns
    Alexander the Great's campaigns into India bring Greek cultural and artistic influences to the region.
    Reference: https://www.britannica.com/biography/Alexander-the-Great

  • 268–232 BCE
    Reign of Ashoka
    Ashoka's rule leads to the promotion of Dharma and Buddhism, impacting Hinduism.
    Promotion of Dharma
    Emperor Ashoka promotes Dharma (moral law) and Buddhism across his empire, impacting Hindu practices and encouraging tolerance.
    Reference: https://www.britannica.com/biography/Ashoka

  • 52 CE–500 AD
    Introduction of Christianity
    Christianity is introduced to India, with early presence by Thomas the Apostle and formal establishment by the 6th century AD.
    Arrival of Christianity
    Christianity reaches India through early missionaries such as Thomas the Apostle and is formally established by the 6th century AD.
    Reference: https://www.britannica.com/topic/Christianity

  • 200 AD
    Codification of Hindu Laws
    Hindu laws are codified, marking a significant development in the legal and social aspects of Hinduism.
    Legal Codification
    The codification of Hindu laws includes texts such as the Manusmriti, which outlines social and legal codes.
    Reference: https://www.britannica.com/topic/Manusmriti

  • 320–500 AD
    Gupta Empire
    The Gupta Empire's rise ushers in a period of stability and prosperity, leading to the consolidation of Hindu traditions and Sanskrit literature.
    Golden Age of Hindu Culture
    The Gupta Empire is known for its contributions to Hindu culture, including the consolidation of traditions and Sanskrit literature.
    Reference: https://www.britannica.com/place/Gupta-dynasty

  • 500 AD
    Fall of Gupta Empire
    The Gupta Empire falls, leading to the fragmentation of India into weaker entities.
    End of Gupta Rule
    The fall of the Gupta Empire leads to the fragmentation of India into smaller regional kingdoms.
    Reference: https://www.britannica.com/place/Gupta-dynasty

  • 788 AD
    Birth of Adi Sankaracharya
    Adi Sankaracharya, a significant philosopher and scholar, is born, introducing important monotheistic beliefs into Hinduism.
    Birth of Adi Sankaracharya
    Adi Sankaracharya, a key figure in Advaita Vedanta philosophy, is born in Kerala, India.
    Reference: https://www.britannica.com/biography/Shankara

  • 800–1000 AD
    Bhakti Movements Rise
    The Bhakti movement emphasizes devotion to God, responding to various pressures and reforming Hindu practices.
    Emergence of Bhakti Movement
    The Bhakti movement emphasizes personal devotion to deities and becomes a significant aspect of Hindu practice.
    Reference: https://www.britannica.com/topic/Bhakti

  • 900 AD
    Shankara’s Monotheism
    Shankara introduces the concept of one Brahman, bringing significant monotheistic beliefs into Hinduism.
    Advaita Vedanta Philosophy
    Shankara's Advaita Vedanta philosophy emphasizes the non-duality of Brahman and the illusory nature of the material world.
    Reference: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Advaita_Vedanta

  • 950 AD
    Queen Sembiyan Madhavi
    Queen Sembiyan Madhavi dedicates her life to building Hindu temples and monasteries.
    Temple Construction
    Queen Sembiyan Madhavi is known for her significant contributions to building Hindu temples and monasteries in South India.
    Reference: https://www.indianhistory.org/queensembiyanmadhavi.html

  • 600 CE–1100 AD
    Arrival of Islam in India
    Islam arrives in India through trade and invasions, including the introduction of Shia Islam and Turkic invasions.
    Introduction of Islam
    Islam is introduced to India through trade, invasions, and the establishment of various Islamic dynasties.
    Reference: https://www.britannica.com/topic/History-of-Islam

  • 1400–1700 AD
    European Arrival
    Europeans arrive in India, impacting Hindu culture and society with their colonial ambitions.
    Arrival of Europeans
    European colonizers, including the Portuguese, Dutch, French, and British, arrive in India, influencing local cultures and politics.
    Reference: https://www.britannica.com/place/India/Colonial-period

  • 1542 AD
    Mogul Emperor Akbar
    Akbar's efforts to unite Muslims and Hindus highlight religious tolerance and reform.
    Religious Tolerance of Akbar
    Mogul Emperor Akbar is known for his policy of religious tolerance and efforts to integrate Hindus into his administration.
    Reference: https://www.britannica.com/biography/Akbar

  • 1600 AD
    Hindu Renaissance
    The Hindu Renaissance begins, focusing on modern interpretations of Hindu texts and social reform.
    Hindu Renaissance Movement
    The Hindu Renaissance emphasizes a reformulation of Hindu beliefs and practices in response to modern challenges and colonialism.
    Reference: https://www.britannica.com/event/Hindu-Renaissance

  • 1605 AD
    Jahangir’s Rule
    Jahangir’s rule incorporates both Muslim and Hindu teachings.
    Jahangir’s Religious Policies
    Emperor Jahangir incorporates Hindu practices and teachings into his rule, reflecting a syncretic approach to governance.
    Reference: https://www.britannica.com/biography/Jahangir

  • 1669 AD
    Aurangzeb Outlaws Hindu Worship
    Aurangzeb outlaws Hindu worship, leading to forced conversions.
    Aurangzeb’s Policies
    Emperor Aurangzeb enforces policies that outlaw Hindu worship and impose restrictions on Hindu practices.
    Reference: https://www.britannica.com/biography/Aurangzeb

  • 1757 AD
    Battle of Plassey
    Robert Clive's victory at the Battle of Plassey ends the Mogul Empire and establishes British dominance.
    Battle of Plassey
    Robert Clive's victory at the Battle of Plassey marks the beginning of British dominance in India, leading to significant political and social changes.
    Reference: https://www.britannica.com/event/Battle-of-Plassey

  • 1861 AD
    British Raj Criminalizes Homosexuality
    Section 377 of the Indian Penal Code criminalizes homosexual activities under British rule.
    Criminalization of Homosexuality
    The British Raj enacts Section 377 of the Indian Penal Code, criminalizing homosexual activities.
    Reference: https://www.britannica.com/topic/Section-377

  • 1894 AD
    Paramahansa Ramakrishna
    Ramakrishna inspires the Ramakrishna Order; Swami Vivekananda introduces Hinduism to the West.
    Foundation of Ramakrishna Order
    Paramahansa Ramakrishna inspires the establishment of the Ramakrishna Order, focusing on spiritual practice and religious unity.
    Reference: https://www.britannica.com/biography/Ramakrishna

    Swami Vivekananda’s Global Influence
    Swami Vivekananda introduces Hinduism to the Western world, promoting spiritual and philosophical ideas.
    Reference: https://www.britannica.com/biography/Vivekananda

  • 1960s AD
    Migration to the West
    Hindu migrate to Britain and North America; Transcendental Meditation and Hare Krishna movement gain global popularity.
    Hindu Migration to the West
    During the 1960s, Hindus migrate to Western countries, bringing their religious and cultural practices with them.
    Reference: https://www.britannica.com/topic/Indian-diaspora

  • 1979 AD
    Hindu Temple in Manitoba
    The Hindu Society of Manitoba opens its first Hindu Temple and Cultural Centre in Winnipeg.
    Opening of Hindu Temple in Manitoba
    The Hindu Society of Manitoba opens its first Hindu temple, marking a significant milestone for the Hindu community in Canada.
    Reference: https://www.hindusocietyofmanitoba.org/about-us

  • 1990s–Present
    Global Hindu Communities
    Hindu communities expand globally; significant temples are built around the world.
    Expansion of Hindu Communities
    Hindu communities continue to expand globally, establishing significant temples and cultural centers worldwide.
    Reference: https://www.britannica.com/topic/Hinduism

  • 1997 AD
    Deepak Obhrai and K. R. Narayanan
    Deepak Obhrai becomes the first Hindu-Canadian elected to Canadian Parliament; K. R. Narayanan becomes President of India.
    Deepak Obhrai’s Election
    Deepak Obhrai is elected as the first Hindu-Canadian Member of Parliament in Canada.
    Reference: https://www.parl.ca/biography.aspx?Language=E&Mode=1&Parl=37&Ses=2&ID=2126

    K. R. Narayanan’s Presidency
    K. R. Narayanan is elected as the President of India, becoming the first Dalit to hold the office.
    Reference: https://www.britannica.com/biography/K-R-Narayanan

  • 2001 AD
    Kumbh Mela Festival
    The Kumbh Mela Festival is recognized as the largest religious gathering in the world.
    Kumbh Mela Festival
    The Kumbh Mela is recognized as the largest religious gathering in the world, attracting millions of pilgrims.
    Reference: https://www.britannica.com/event/Kumbh-Mela

  • 2007 AD
    Shri Swaminarayan Mandir in Toronto
    The Shri Swaminarayan Mandir is consecrated in Toronto, representing a significant Hindu temple in Canada.
    Consecration of Shri Swaminarayan Mandir
    The Shri Swaminarayan Mandir in Toronto is consecrated, marking a major Hindu religious and cultural milestone in Canada.
    Reference: https://www.shriSwaminarayanToronto.org/
